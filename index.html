About us

We build with society, not just for it.

TIS Association — Technology and Innovation for Society — is a Romanian non-profit working at the intersection of innovation and the public good.

We believe the best technology is the kind people barely notice, because it simply makes life better. That conviction shapes everything: the projects we take on, the partners we choose, and the way we measure whether we've actually helped.

Founded to put technology to work for the communities that need it most.

  • 01

    Impact over hype

    We measure success by what changes for real people, not by how new the technology sounds.

  • 02

    Built to last

    Solutions designed to be sustainable — environmentally, and for the communities that adopt them.

  • 03

    Open by default

    We collaborate across sectors and share what we learn, so good ideas travel further.

Previous projects

What we've delivered

A look at past initiatives where our approach turned into measurable change on the ground.

Project 01 · Sustainability

EcoHuedin 360

A community-centred initiative driving sustainable change in Huedin — bringing residents, local institutions, and green practices together around a shared environmental goal.

Project 02 · Partnership

EcoAlianța

An alliance bringing partners together around shared environmental and social goals — proof that progress moves faster when organisations pull in the same direction.

Project 03 · Environment

Terra

A project rooted in protecting and restoring the land we all depend on — practical stewardship of the natural systems that keep communities healthy.

Project 04 · 2019–2023 · EU-funded

Technology for Accessible Justice

An EU-funded programme increasing the transparency, quality, and accessibility of the judicial system through technology — widening access to justice services for disadvantaged citizens in multicultural urban areas. TIS was initiator, designer, and project manager.

Project 05 · 2018–2019 · Civic tech

Smart Volunteer (voluntarI)

An alternative public-policy proposal under SCAP to make communities more resilient in emergencies — delivered as the "smart volunteer" (voluntarI) interactive platform, in partnership with the Foundation for SMURD. TIS was initiator, designer, and partner coordinator.
